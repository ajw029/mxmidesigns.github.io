Fitted

Track your progress to see how a little effort goes a long way.

THE PROBLEM

Finding exercise routines for your level can be difficult, especially if you want to try something new and finding routines that fit into your schedule is not easy.


The SOLUTION

I can encourage people who want to exercise get into physical activities by providing easy routines, guides, interactive examples, and info.


TOOLS

Invision (Prototype), Sketch (Wireframes and Visual Design)


View Prototype

DISCOVERY

USER RESEARCH

Getting to know my users' needs.

My research goal was to better understand how people like to work out, what discourages them, and what motivates them to keep going.

OBSERVATIONS

  1. People who are new or returning to fitness want to find activities they like, and get into a good routine.
  2. Users like to search and view routines, guides, daily challenges, and other information on any device. They want to also keep a schedule by adding sessions to their personal calendar.
  3. Users aim to get into a routine that suits them and need an app they can use whenever they like.
  4. When on the go or when practicing exercise routines at home, in the park, on the street, users want to stay fit anywhere

KEY INSIGHTS

  1. Users want to become healthy and enjoy the associated benefits (better mood, weight management, reduce risks of illness, learning something new).
  2. Exercise should be fun and suited to each user
  3. Fitting exercise into daily routines, such as walking or cycling to work or school

PERSONA

USER FLOWS

I created a user flow for the key tasks within the app: search and filter exercise videos, exercise scheduler, add sessions to calendar, create account, tracking progress, daily challenges/rewards, and social sharing.

Responsive Design

Sketching & Brainstorming

Based on the user flow diagram, I sketched out mobile screens that would meet the user stories' requirements.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Using Grids

I created low-fidelity 12 column wireframes for my responsive web app that that convey the key functionality the users would need.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Composition & Visual Hierarchy = More Intuitive

Considering the key action I wanted users to take, I applied visual hierarchy to my web app screens. I focused on size, contrast, boldness, positioning, and then refining my design by applying good spacing. After arranging the elements into a visual hierarchy that makes sense to my users, I made my design more recognizable and clear to users.

Visual Direction

Setting the mood

Before I begin to add my high fidelity design to my wireframe, I created a mood board to communicate the visual direction of my project.

The mood board represents a modern and cool approach to the fitness web app. By focusing on dark colors, bold typography, and simple patterns, the overall app will have a motivating atmosphere to help users keep their determination in working out.

Usability Testing

Is it useful and functinal?

Before applying more details, I wanted to make sure that each screen's navigation and the basic functionality was user friendly.

GOALS + METHODOLOGY:

  1. The aim of the test is to take a closer look at the main functionalities of the app to determine its overall usefulness.
  2. Identify any problems in order to improve these elements prior further testing.
  3. I hope to understand my user's needs and gain new clarity regarding the navigation of the current app prototype.
  4. The usability tests will be conducted moderated in-peron. Participants will use my iphone 8+

USER TESTING TASKS:

  1. Create new account.
  2. Add new study sets.
  3. Create new flashcards to study sets.
  4. Review and test new learnings.
Prioritized Issues To Be Addressed
Issue Severity Suggested Change Evidence
Finding study sets quickly. HIGH Add a search bar so users can access their study sets quicker. Users wanted to find their study set without scrolling down too much.
Users find that there were too many clicks to get to add flashcards. HIGH Make the next destination screen to be the same study set so users can easily add flash cards next. After creating a new study set, users prefer to stay in the same folder to add words faster.
Missing dictation feature LOW Add a dictation feature. Users asked if they can also use their voice to enter text.
Bookmarking HIGH Have a starred feature where users can bookmark the study sets they need to focus more on. Users want the ability to highlight or bookmark study sets that they have the most trouble with.
Editing MEDIUM Have an edit flashcards option so users can quickly edit as they review Users wanted to search quickly and edit words along the way.

Iteration
